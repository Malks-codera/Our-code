Endangered Animals of

Jharkhand

Ganges River Dolphin

The Ganges river dolphin (Platanista gangesica gangetica) lives in freshwater rivers. Its population is 1200 to 1800. Its conservation status is endangered. It is the National Aquatic Animal of India. Some threats to the Ganges river dolphin are hunting, and pollution.

Asian Elephant

The Asian elephant (Elephas maximus indicus) lives in forests. It is the largest land mammal on the continent of Asia. Its population is fewer than 50000. They live in South and Southeast Asia. Bornean, Sri Lankan, Sumatran and Indian Elephants are all Asian Elephants. Its conservation status is endangered. Some threats to the Asian elephant are habitat loss and poaching.

Sloth Bear

The sloth bear (Melursus ursinus) that is native to the Indian subcontinent. Its conservation status is vulnerable. There are about 20000 or fewer sloth bears left in the wild. Some reasons why its population has decreased are hunting and habitat destruction.

Conservation Efforts

The Supreme Court that prohibited mining activities within National Parks and Sanctuaries and within one kilometer radius from the boundaries of the National Parks and Sanctuaries.
