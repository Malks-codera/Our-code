Endangered Species of

Himachal Pradesh

Western Tragopan

The Western tragopan (Tragopan melanocephalus) or the Western horned tragopan is a pheasant. It is found along the Himalayas. Its conservation status is vulnerable. Its population is declining due to loss and degradation of forest.

Himalayan Musk Deer

The Himalayan musk deer (Moschus leucogaster), also known as the white-bellied musk deer is found in the Himalayan mountain range. Its conservation status is endangered. Some reasons why the area in which they are found has reduced is due to trapping by humans and habitat destruction.

Cheer Pheasant

The cheer pheasant (Catreus wallichii) is also known as Wallich's pheasant or chir pheasant. It is a large pheasant with a long tail. It is found along steep, grassy hillsides in the Himalayas. Its conservation status is vulnerable. Some reasons why it is endangered are hunting and habitat loss.

Conservation Efforts

Some of the Himachal Pradesh Forest Department's projects are Project Snow Leopard, Western Tragopan Conservation Programme, and Cheer Pheasant Conservation.
